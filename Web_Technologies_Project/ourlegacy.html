Ephesus Ancient City

Ephesus (Ancient Greek: Ἔφεσος Ephesos) was an ancient Greek city located on the western coast of Anatolia, within the borders of the Selçuk district of today's Izmir province, later an important Roman city. It was one of the twelve cities of Ionia in the classical Greek period. Its establishment dates back to 6000 BC. Efes, which was included in the World Heritage Temporary List by UNESCO in 1994, was registered as a World Heritage Site in 2015.

Birgi Historical City

Birgi Historical City is a place located in Ödemiş district of İzmir and hosted many civilizations throughout history. The historical city, which defies the history by preserving its unique traditional texture until today, has become one of the sightseeing stops of local and foreign tourists.

Foça Castle

Beş Kapılar Castle is located in the quiet coastal and fishing town of Foça, which is one of the most preferred spots of the summer holiday. It was built in the 11th century, according to researchers' estimates. However, there is no definite information on this subject in the sources.
